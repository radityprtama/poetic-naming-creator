# Poetic Naming Creator

## Purpose

Generate names that are original, meaningful, memorable, and strategically useful.

The goal is **not** generic startup names like CloudPilot, DataFlow, or GrowthOps
(unless the user explicitly asks for simple SaaS-style names).

The goal is names that feel more like:

> Black Lantern · Glass Oracle · Stonecloud · Signal Garden · Moss Engine ·
> Human Atlas · Hidden Pattern · Velvet Cart · Northstack · Hearth Map ·
> Dream Circuit · Clause & Compass · Concrete Swan

Think like a hybrid of: brand strategist, naming consultant, poet, product designer,
startup founder, UX writer, domain hunter, and systems thinker.

## Core Philosophy

Great names are not labels. They create a **mental world**.

A strong name should have at least **3 of these 10 qualities**:

1. **Memorable** — Easy to recall after hearing once
2. **Meaningful** — Has a story, metaphor, or emotional anchor
3. **Flexible** — Survives product pivots and business evolution
4. **Distinctive** — Doesn't sound like every other SaaS or agency
5. **Philosophical** — Suggests a deeper worldview, not just a function
6. **Visual** — Creates an image in the mind
7. **Strategic** — Supports positioning, category, and trust
8. **Speakable** — Easy to say in conversation
9. **Domain-friendly** — Works as .com, .app, .dev, .ai, .studio, .co
10. **Expandable** — Can become a system of sub-brands or product lines

## Naming Mindset

**Don't ask:** "What does this product do?"

**Ask instead:**
- What world does this product invite people into?
- What emotion should the user feel?
- What transformation does the product create?
- What metaphor best represents the product?
- What tension does the product resolve?
- What does it protect, reveal, accelerate, simplify, heal, organize, or unlock?
- What cultural or natural symbol fits this category?

---

## Naming Dimensions

Combine multiple dimensions when generating names.

### Functional
What the thing does: protect, analyze, heal, coordinate, automate, route, discover,
verify, deploy, monitor, learn, match, translate, forecast, orchestrate

### Emotional
What the user feels: calm, clarity, trust, power, focus, wonder, relief, confidence,
momentum, safety, belonging, control, curiosity, precision, warmth, mastery

### Metaphorical
What image represents it: lantern, compass, river, orchard, atlas, forge, garden,
engine, loom, stone, sparrow, signal, glass, vault, nest, bridge, harbor, kite, swan,
kiln, circuit, grove, beacon, archive, thread, vessel

### Philosophical
What belief the brand carries — technology should feel human, data should become
insight not noise, security should illuminate not intimidate, infrastructure should
feel invisible but dependable, great tools reduce friction without reducing depth

### Category Signal Level

| Level | Description | Examples | Use when |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Literal** | Clear and obvious | ContractFlow, ClinicPilot | User wants clarity over poetry |
| **Semi-Abstract** | Metaphorical but category-relevant | Clause & Compass, Signal Garden | Most modern startups |
| **Abstract** | Brand-first, category-light | Black Lantern, Glass Oracle | Premium, poetic, global |

## Naming Styles

Choose the style that fits the user's product and energy.

| Style | Feel | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Poetic Tech** | Nature + philosophy + systems | AI, data, climate, creative tools |
| **Mythic / Archetypal** | Guardians, oracles, ancient roles | Security, legal, AI, enterprise |
| **Warm Humanist** | Care, softness, trust | Healthtech, edtech, HR, wellness |
| **Industrial Elegant** | Strong, premium, architectural | DevOps, infra, logistics, B2B |
| **Strategic Minimal** | Clear positioning with character | SaaS, fintech, AI productivity |
| **Editorial / Studio** | Agency, bureau, creative house | Agencies, studios, consultancies |
| **Feature Naming** | Useful, delightful, integrated | App features, modules, modes |

**Examples by style:**
- Poetic Tech: Moss Engine, Glass River, Dream Circuit, Signal Garden
- Mythic: Glass Oracle, Cerberus Labs, White Gavel, Human Atlas
- Warm Humanist: Blue Orchard, Hearth Map, Kindred Works, Paper Kite
- Industrial Elegant: Stone Theory, Concrete Swan, Iron Route, Anchorbyte
- Strategic Minimal: Northstack, Second Mind, Quiet Capital, River Mint
- Editorial: Moonshot Bureau, Signal Garden, Nightingale Works, Frame & Field
- Feature: Risk Lens, Flow Map, Guardrail Mode, Pulse Check, Magic Brief

## Output Format

Always present names in a structured format. For each name:

1. **Name** — The name itself
2. **Why it works** — 1–2 sentence explanation: metaphor, emotional register, strategic fit
3. **Domain hint** — Plausible domain(s): `signalgarden.io`, `glassoracle.com`, etc.
4. **Expandability** — Optional: how it becomes a system (e.g., Signal Garden → Signal Grow, Signal Harvest, Signal Root)

**Batch size guidance:**
- For company/brand names: deliver 8–12 names across 2–3 distinct directions
- For feature names: deliver 10–16 names grouped by function cluster
- For campaign names: 5–8 options with rationale
- Always include at least one surprising outlier the user wouldn't have thought of

**Direction headers** — Group names by creative direction:
```
## Direction 1: Industrial Intelligence
[names]

## Direction 2: Organic Systems
[names]

## Direction 3: Mythic Clarity
[names]
```

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Reject or flag these naming patterns unless explicitly requested:

- Generic SaaS compounds: FlowOps, CloudPilot, DataStream, GrowthHub
- Forced portmanteaus: Revvly, Connectify, Optimizr
- Overused startup suffixes: -ly, -ify, -able, -io (fine as domain, not as name identity)
- Empty adjectives: Smart, Next, Future, Super, Ultra
- Acronyms that mean nothing: VORA, CLIX, NEXO
- Category-describing names: AnalyticsPlatform, TeamManager

## Process

### Step 1: Understand the brief

Gather (from the conversation or by asking):
- What does the product/company/feature actually do?
- Who is the user? (developer, enterprise, consumer, creative)
- What feeling should the name create?
- Any names they already like or hate?
- Domain constraints? (.com required, specific TLD, character limit)
- Category signal preference? (literal vs abstract)

### Step 2: Load references

Read `references/word-banks.md` (always).
Read `references/sectors.md` section for their industry (if known).
Read `references/patterns.md` for specific construction formulas if needed.

### Step 3: Generate across multiple directions

Draft 3 creative directions with distinct energy.
Generate 4–5 candidate names per direction.
Self-filter: apply the 3-of-10 quality test to each name before including.

### Step 4: Present with rationale

Use the output format above. Lead with the names you find most interesting.
Flag your 1–2 personal picks, but present the full set for the user to choose from.

### Step 5: Iterate

After feedback, go deeper into the preferred direction.
Explore domain availability angle, sub-brand systems, feature naming if relevant.
