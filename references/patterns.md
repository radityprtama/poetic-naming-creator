# Name Construction Patterns

Ten repeatable patterns for generating strong names. Each has a formula, examples,
and notes on when to use it.

---

## Pattern 1: Object + Object
**Formula:** `[evocative adjective/object] + [symbolic object]`

Creates strong visual metaphor. The two objects shouldn't be literally related — the
tension between them is where meaning lives.

Examples: Black Lantern · Glass River · Paper Kite · Market Fox · Concrete Swan ·
Signal Garden · Dream Circuit · Hearth Map

Best for: Brand names, agency names, premium product names, poetic tech

---

## Pattern 2: Material + System
**Formula:** `[material or nature element] + [technical or system concept]`

Creates strength + technology energy. Grounds abstract tech in something physical.

Examples: Stonecloud · Anchorbyte · Iron Route · Green Kiln · Glass Oracle

Best for: Infrastructure, devtools, AI, climate, analytics, B2B SaaS

---

## Pattern 3: Human Concept + Navigation
**Formula:** `[domain concept] + [navigation or structure metaphor]`

Creates guidance and intelligence energy. Implies "we'll help you find your way."

Examples: Human Atlas · Clause & Compass · Hearth Map · Civic Loom · Talent Compass

Best for: Legal, HR, property, education, knowledge systems, health coordination

---

## Pattern 4: Nature + Machine
**Formula:** `[nature symbol] + [machine, data, or system symbol]`

Creates organic technology energy. Suggests intelligence that grows rather than grinds.

Examples: Moss Engine · Orchard Grid · Soil & Signal · River Mint · PlantPulse

Best for: Climate, agriculture, AI, health, sustainability, data products

---

## Pattern 5: Color + Symbol
**Formula:** `[color with semantic weight] + [symbolic object]`

Creates immediate visual identity and emotional positioning. Only use colors that carry
meaning (see word-banks.md color table).

Examples: Black Lantern · Blue Orchard · White Gavel · Red Compass · Gold Thread

Best for: Memorable agency names, product names, security products

---

## Pattern 6: Abstract Role
**Formula:** `[quality or material] + [role, animal, or archetypal entity]`

Creates a character-like identity. The product becomes a persona, not just a tool.

Examples: Glass Oracle · Market Fox · Iron Sparrow · Second Mind · Quiet Sentinel

Best for: AI agents, security, fintech, marketing intelligence, productivity tools

---

## Pattern 7: Pairing with Ampersand
**Formula:** `[domain noun] & [metaphor noun]`

Creates studio, editorial, or premium-partnership feel. Implies depth and complementarity.

Examples: Clause & Compass · Frame & Field · Soil & Signal · Pulse & Petal · Stone & Thread

Best for: Agencies, studios, product suites, premium B2B tools, professional services

---

## Pattern 8: Place or Institution
**Formula:** `[evocative concept] + [place type or institution type]`

Creates a world, not just a product. The user belongs to something, not just uses something.

Examples: Moonshot Bureau · Lantern School · Nightingale Works · Signal Garden · Kite Academy

Best for: Agencies, platforms, communities, education products, creative studios

---

## Pattern 9: Hidden Insight
**Formula:** `[hidden / quiet / deep / still] + [insight, value, or system noun]`

Creates mystery and intelligence energy. Implies the product sees what others miss.

Examples: Hidden Pattern · Signal Ash · Deep Current · Quiet Capital · Dark Compass · Still Water

Best for: Analytics, finance, research platforms, AI, intelligence products

---

## Pattern 10: Feature Utility
**Formula:** `[benefit or context] + [interaction metaphor]`

Creates clear, useful, and delightful feature names that feel native to a product.
Should feel like they belong inside a specific tool, not as standalone brands.

Examples: Risk Lens · Flow Map · Guardrail Mode · Signal View · Pulse Check · Magic Brief ·
          Smart Cart · Deploy Gate · Talent Map · Clause Lens

Best for: Feature names inside products, dashboard sections, settings modes, power-user flows

---

## Mixing Patterns

The strongest names often blend two patterns. Examples:

| Name | Primary pattern | Secondary pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Black Lantern | Color + Symbol (#5) | Object + Object (#1) |
| Stonecloud | Material + System (#2) | Object + Object (#1) |
| Clause & Compass | Ampersand (#7) | Human + Navigation (#3) |
| Moss Engine | Nature + Machine (#4) | Material + System (#2) |
| Glass Oracle | Abstract Role (#6) | Material + System (#2) |

---

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these construction patterns:

| Anti-pattern | Example | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Verb + -ly | Revvly, Growthly | Forgettable, dated |
| Random consonant cluster | Vora, Nexo, Clix | No meaning, no image |
| Category + generic | TeamManager, FlowOps | Describes, doesn't brand |
| Overloaded portmanteau | Connectify, Optimizr | Sounds desperate |
| All-caps acronym | VIRA, CLOX | Corporate, cold, non-visual |
